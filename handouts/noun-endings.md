<p><sup>1</sup>Stems that end with ν, ρ, ς, or οντ add no ending for the nominative singular. The vowel before the final consonant often lengthens. ε becomes η. o becomes ω.</p>
<p><sup>2</sup>The final stem consonant sometimes changes. Final τ is often dropped.</p>
<p><sup>3</sup>Sometimes ος lengthens to ως.</p>
<p><sup>4</sup>For 1st and 2nd declension nouns, this ι is subscript.</p>
<p><sup>5</sup>Third declension nouns end in a consonant, so the ι cannot become subscript.</p>
<p><sup>6</sup>The vowel before the final consonant often changes.<p></p>
<p><sup>7</sup>This α merges with the final vowel.</p>
<p><sup>8</sup>The ω merges with the final vowel.</p>
